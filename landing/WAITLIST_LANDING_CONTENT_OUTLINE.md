# Meridian Waitlist Landing Page Content Outline

## Positioning

Meridian is not a generic personal finance app.

It is a FIRE planning app for people who want work to become optional.

Core message:

```text
Make your FIRE date visible.
```

Supporting message:

```text
Meridian turns your spending, savings, investments, and cash flow into a live FIRE plan, so you can see when work becomes optional and what moves the date closer.
```

Primary audience:

- People in the FIRE community
- People actively tracking savings rate, net worth, and early retirement progress
- People who use spreadsheets, FIRE calculators, budgeting apps, or manual tracking but still do not have one live view of their path

Conversion goal:

- Get users to join the private iOS beta waitlist

Page rules:

- Say who this is for in the hero.
- Say the problem directly.
- Use 3 to 5 concrete pain points and solutions.
- Show product proof with real screenshots.
- Keep text scannable. No report-like paragraphs.
- Use clear CTA buttons throughout.
- Use motion only to explain the product, not as decoration.

## Page Structure

Recommended final structure:

1. Hero: Built for the FIRE community
2. Problem: FIRE needs more than a budget app
3. Core Product: Live FIRE countdown
4. Spending Clarity: Needs and Wants
5. Goal-Driven Budgeting: Budget backward from freedom
6. Smart Savings Tracking: Savings rate as the main lever
7. Unified FIRE View: Spending, savings, and investments together
8. What-If Simulator: Test tradeoffs before living them
9. How It Works: Manual first, sync later
10. Trust: Private, read-only planning
11. Final CTA: Join the FIRE beta
12. FAQ

## Motion Principles

Use motion for product explanation:

- A date changes.
- A savings amount increases.
- A progress bar fills.
- Transactions sort into categories.
- Screens slide in a carousel.

Avoid motion that hurts conversion:

- Too many decorative animations.
- Slow page load.
- Huge video assets before the CTA loads.
- Abstract animations that do not explain the product.
- Motion that delays reading the headline or using the waitlist form.
